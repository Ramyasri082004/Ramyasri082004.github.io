Cardiac Health Dashboard: Analyzing Key Indicators for Heart Attack Diagnosis

Built an interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing **70,000+ patient records** to identify key factors affecting cardiac health. This project applies data cleaning, KPI design, and visualization to generate actionable healthcare insights for prevention and awareness.

Project Visuals

Goal

Understand demographic, lifestyle, and medical patterns linked to cardiac conditions and enable preventive healthcare decisions.

Key KPIs

  • Total Patients: 70,000+
  • Heart Disease Cases: 33.3%
  • Average Age: 52 years
  • Male vs Female Patients: 51% vs 49%

Key Insights

  • Age & Gender: Heart disease prevalence increases significantly after 50 years. Males are slightly more prone compared to females.
  • Blood Pressure & Cholesterol: Patients with hypertension and high cholesterol have ~2x higher risk of cardiac disease.
  • Smoking & Lifestyle: Smokers show higher cardiac risk (39%) vs non-smokers (28%). Sedentary lifestyle strongly correlates with poor cardiac health.
  • BMI & Obesity: Overweight/obese patients (BMI > 30) contribute to nearly 45% of heart disease cases.
  • Diabetes: Cardiac disease prevalence in diabetic patients is 41% compared to 29% in non-diabetics.

Recommendations

  • Promote lifestyle changes: physical activity, reduced smoking & alcohol consumption.
  • Target preventive checkups for patients aged 45+ with hypertension/cholesterol.
  • Encourage weight management and diet awareness programs.
  • Design early-intervention programs for diabetic patients.
  • Use predictive modeling for early detection of high-risk groups.

Tools & Techniques

  • Power BI for interactive dashboards & storytelling
  • Data Cleaning with Python (Pandas, NumPy)
  • DAX for KPI measures (Heart Disease %, Age Groups, Risk Segmentation)
  • Healthcare Analytics for preventive insights

Final Note: This project showcases how healthcare data can be leveraged to uncover patterns in cardiac disease prevalence, providing hospitals and policymakers with insights to improve patient care and reduce risk factors.
